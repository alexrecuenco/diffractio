Inteferences by a biprism¶

A classical experiment in Optics is the interference process by a biprism. In order to obtain a good inteference process a monocromatic light beam needs to be divided into two coherent waves and join them again. This can be performed using a Fresnel biprism.

In order to analyze the inteference process we can use the XZ formalism or the XY.

XY formalism¶

In the first place, we create a plane wave and mask it with a circular mask.

u0.plane_wave()

t0 = Scalar_mask_XY(x=x0, y=y0, wavelength=wavelength)
t0.circle(r0=(0 * um, 0 * um), radius=(75 * um, 75 * um))

After that, we generate a Fresnel Biprism mask. The field just after the Fresnel biprism is just the multiplication of the field by the two masks.

In [4]:
t1 = Scalar_mask_XY(x=x0, y=y0, wavelength=wavelength)
t1.biprism_fresnel(
    r0=(0 * um, 0 * um), width=100 * um, height=5 * um, n=1.5)

u1 = t0 * t1 * u0

Then, we create a function in order to draw the intensity distribution after the mask

In [5]:
def difraccion_biprism_Fresnel(u1, z_mask):
    """Computes and draws the intensity distribution of a light beam after a mask.
    
    Parameters:
    u1 (Scalar_field_XY): Field just after the mask.
    z_mask (float): Distance from the mask."""

    if z_mask>0:
        u2 = u1.RS(z=z_mask, new_field=True)
    else:
        u2 = u1
        
    u2.draw(kind='intensity')
    plt.title("$z={:2.2f}\,um$".format(z_mask), fontsize=24)
